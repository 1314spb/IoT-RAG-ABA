- Domain: Independent and Self-help
- Task: Laundry and Personal Hygiene
- Sub task: Washing and Drying Clothes
- Description: Teach the client to independently wash and dry clothes, with minimal support from the therapist or caregiver.
- Materials: Laundry basket, washing machine, dryer, detergent, fabric softener
- Procedure: 
  1. Begin by teaching the client to sort clothes into different categories (e.g. lights, darks, delicates).
  2. Practice loading the washing machine and dryer with the correct amount of clothes.
  3. Teach the client to use the washing machine and dryer, including selecting the correct cycle and temperature.
  4. Gradually increase the complexity of the task by introducing new laundry items, such as towels or bedding.
  5. Use visual supports such as pictures or a laundry app on a tablet to aid the client.
- Data Collection: 
  1. Track the number of loads of laundry the client is able to complete independently.
  2. Measure the time it takes for the client to complete the laundry task.
  3. Assess the client's ability to sort clothes correctly and use the washing machine and dryer correctly.
- Variations: 
  1. Practice doing laundry for multiple people in the household.
  2. Introduce new laundry items, such as delicate or special-care items.
  3. Increase the amount of laundry the client has to complete in a single session.
- Reinforcement: 
  1. Praise the client for their efforts and accomplishments.
  2. Use a token economy system to reward the client for completing the laundry task.
  3. Provide a choice of clean clothes or personal care items as a reward for successful completion of the task.
- Skills Developed: 
  1. Independent living skills
  2. Problem-solving skills
  3. Laundry skills
  4. Time management skills
  5. Self-reliance
