- Domain: Social Emotion
- Task: Initiating and Maintaining Conversations
- Sub task: Taking Turns in a Conversation
- Description: This task aims to teach the client to initiate and maintain conversations by taking turns and engaging in reciprocal communication.
- Materials: 
  - Conversation cards or pictures
  - Mirrors or reflective surfaces
  - Simple conversation starters (e.g., "How was your day?")
- Procedure:
  1. Begin by teaching the client to take turns in a simple conversation, such as asking a question and waiting for a response.
  2. Use mirrors or reflective surfaces to help the client practice taking turns and observing the other person's responses.
  3. Gradually introduce more complex conversation starters and scenarios, such as asking open-ended questions or engaging in a discussion.
  4. Use reinforcement techniques, such as praise and positive reinforcement, to encourage the client to engage in reciprocal communication.
- Data Collection:
  - Observe the client's ability to initiate and maintain conversations by taking turns and engaging in reciprocal communication.
  - Track the client's progress in using conversation starters and responding to others.
  - Use rating scales or checklists to assess the client's ability to take turns and engage in conversations.
- Variations:
  - For clients with more advanced skills, introduce more complex conversation topics and scenarios.
  - For clients with more basic skills, use simpler conversation starters and focus on taking turns.
  - Use different types of materials, such as videos or role-playing, to provide more diverse and engaging opportunities for practicing conversations.
- Reinforcement:
  - Praise the client for their efforts and progress in initiating and maintaining conversations.
  - Use positive reinforcement, such as stickers or stars, to encourage the client to engage in reciprocal communication.
  - Gradually phase out reinforcement as the client becomes more proficient in taking turns and engaging in conversations.
- Skills Developed:
  - Initiating and maintaining conversations
  - Taking turns in a conversation
  - Engaging in reciprocal communication
