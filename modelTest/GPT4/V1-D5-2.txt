Domain: Independent and Self-help
Task: Buttoning and Unbuttoning Clothing
Sub task: Practicing buttoning a shirt
Description: The child will learn to independently button and unbutton their shirt, promoting self-care and independence in dressing.
Materials: A shirt with large buttons, mirror (optional)
Procedure:
Provide the child with a shirt and demonstrate how to button and unbutton it.
Break the task into smaller steps (e.g., aligning the button with the hole, pushing the button through).
Allow the child to practice with guidance as needed.
Gradually reduce assistance as the child becomes more proficient.
Data Collection: Record the number of successful buttoning/unbuttoning attempts within a session.
Variations:
Use shirts with different button sizes.
Practice on different types of clothing (e.g., jackets, pants).
Reinforcement: Offer praise, stickers, or a preferred activity after each successful buttoning/unbuttoning.
Skills Developed: Fine motor skills, independence in dressing, self-care skills, and hand-eye coordination.
