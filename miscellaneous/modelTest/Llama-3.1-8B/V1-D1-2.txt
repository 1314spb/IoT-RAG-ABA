- Domain: Academic and Learning
- Task: "Following a Recipe"
- Sub task: Following a recipe to complete a simple cooking task, such as making a sandwich or a simple snack.
- Description: This task aims to improve the individual's ability to follow directions, complete a multi-step task, and develop independence in meal preparation.
- Materials: 
  - A simple recipe (e.g., making a peanut butter and jelly sandwich)
  - Ingredients and materials needed for the recipe
  - A visual aid (e.g., picture or chart) of the recipe steps
- Procedure:
  1. Begin by presenting the individual with the recipe and explaining the steps involved.
  2. Break down the recipe into smaller steps and have the individual complete each step.
  3. Provide feedback and guidance as needed to ensure the individual is completing each step correctly.
  4. Gradually increase the complexity of the recipe and the number of steps involved.
- Data Collection:
  - Observe the individual's ability to follow the recipe and complete each step accurately.
  - Record the number of correct and incorrect steps completed.
  - Take note of any challenges or difficulties the individual encounters.
- Variations:
  - Use different recipes with varying levels of complexity.
  - Increase the number of ingredients or steps involved.
  - Have the individual complete the recipe independently without assistance.
  - Use different types of recipes (e.g., baking, cooking, preparing a meal).
- Reinforcement:
  - Provide praise and encouragement for completing each step accurately.
  - Use verbal or visual rewards (e.g., stickers, stars) for completing each step correctly.
  - Gradually phase out reinforcement as the individual becomes more accurate and independent.
- Skills Developed:
  - Following directions and instructions
  - Completing a multi-step task
  - Independence in meal preparation
  - Fine motor skills (e.g., cutting, spreading, assembling)
