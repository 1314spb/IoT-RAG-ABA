- Domain: Social Emotion
- Task: Emotion Recognition and Expression
- Sub task: Matching Facial Expressions to Emotions
- Description: The child learns to identify and match different facial expressions with corresponding emotions, enhancing their ability to recognize and understand various emotional states.
- Materials: Emotion cards with pictures of facial expressions, emotion word cards
- Procedure:
  1. Present the child with a set of emotion cards showing different facial expressions.
  2. Introduce emotion word cards (e.g., happy, sad, angry, surprised).
  3. Ask the child to match the facial expression cards with the correct emotion word cards.
  4. Gradually increase the complexity by introducing more subtle emotions.
  5. Practice expressing these emotions through role-play or mirror exercises.
- Data Collection: Record the number of correct matches and expressions produced by the child in each session.
- Variations:
  1. Use digital images or videos instead of physical cards.
  2. Incorporate body language along with facial expressions.
  3. Use real-life scenarios to contextualize emotions.
- Reinforcement: Provide verbal praise and small rewards for correct matches and accurate expressions.
- Skills Developed: Emotion recognition, empathy, social awareness, nonverbal communication
