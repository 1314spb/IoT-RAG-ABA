- Domain: Communication
- Task: Expanding Expressive Language
- Sub task: Using Two-Word Combinations
- Description: Teaching the client to combine two words to express wants, needs, or observations
- Materials: Picture cards of objects and actions, data collection sheets, reinforcers
- Procedure:
  1. Present two picture cards (e.g., "car" and "go")
  2. Model the two-word combination ("car go")
  3. Prompt the client to repeat the combination
  4. Gradually fade prompts as the client improves
  5. Reinforce successful two-word combinations
  6. Introduce new combinations systematically
- Data Collection: Record the number of correct two-word combinations per session, noting prompt level required
- Variations:
  1. Use real objects instead of picture cards
  2. Incorporate different word combinations (e.g., adjective-noun, verb-noun)
  3. Practice during naturalistic play scenarios
- Reinforcement: Immediate access to requested item/action, praise, or token system
- Skills Developed: Expressive language, word combination, vocabulary expansion, basic sentence structure
