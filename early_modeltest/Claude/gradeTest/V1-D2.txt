- Domain: Social Emotion
- Task: Greetings: Responding
- Subtask: Verbal response to greetings
- Description: Teaching the child to appropriately respond to various greetings in social situations
- Materials: None required, but picture cards of different greeting scenarios can be helpful
- Procedure:
  1. Therapist approaches the child and says a greeting (e.g., "Hello", "Hi", "Good morning")
  2. Prompt the child to respond appropriately (e.g., "Hello", "Hi", "Good morning to you too")
  3. Gradually fade prompts as the child becomes more proficient
  4. Practice with different greetings and in various settings
- Data Collection: Record the number of correct responses out of total opportunities
- Variations:
  - Use different tones of voice for greetings
  - Practice with various people (therapist, parents, peers)
  - Incorporate non-verbal greetings (wave, handshake)
- Reinforcement: Praise, high-fives, or small rewards for correct responses
- Skills Developed:
  - Social awareness
  - Verbal communication
  - Turn-taking in conversation
  - Appropriate social etiquette
